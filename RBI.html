What is RBI

INSPECTION
DRIVEN BY
ACTUAL RISK

Risk-Based Inspection (RBI), standardized under API 580 & API 581, replaces fixed-calendar schedules with a risk-driven approach that determines when, where, and how intensively to inspect each asset.

Risk is the product of Probability of Failure (PoF) and Consequence of Failure (CoF). Equipment with high risk gets more frequent, thorough inspection; low-risk assets can safely have extended intervals — freeing resources and cutting costs without compromising safety.

RBI applies across all process equipment: storage tanks, pressure vessels, separators, distillation columns, heat exchangers, pumps, and fired heaters.

API 581 — Risk-Based Inspection Methodology

The industry standard from the American Petroleum Institute providing a semi-quantitative framework for assessing probability and consequence of failure. Globally adopted across refineries, petrochemical plants, and upstream facilities.

MODULE 01 · PoF

Probability of Failure

Derived from generic failure frequency \(g_{ff}\), a damage factor \(D_f(t)\) for active degradation and inspection history, and a management system factor \(F_{MS}\) reflecting safety program quality. Classified 1–5.

MODULE 02 · CoF

Consequence of Failure

Financial consequence: environmental cleanup \(FC_{environ}\), equipment repair \(FC_{cmd}\), and production loss \(FC_{prod}\). Categorized A–E from <$10K to >$10M based on fluid type, volume, and layout.

MODULE 03 · RLA

Remaining Life Assessment

Per API 653. Uses measured wall thickness \(t_{act}\), corrosion rate \(CR\), and minimum allowable thickness \(t_{min}\) to calculate remaining years. Maximum inspection interval = \(RL/2\) or 15 yr.

THE RISK MATRIX

All equipment is plotted on a 5×5 matrix combining Probability of Failure (1–5) with Consequence of Failure (A–E). The resulting cell drives inspection urgency, method, and frequency.
